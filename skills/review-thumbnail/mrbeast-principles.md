# MrBeast Thumbnail Principles

Sourced from YouTube's interview with **Chucky Appleby**, MrBeast's thumbnail designer, as covered by Social Media Today. MrBeast's team generates ~50 thumbnail/title concepts per video before selecting the best one, and continuously iterates using YouTube's A/B testing feature.

## A. Simplicity & Story

A thumbnail must tell a story at a glance within seconds. Complex or overloaded images don't work. Ask yourself: "Would I click on this?"

- Is the concept immediately clear?
- Can you understand what the video is about in under 2 seconds?
- Is there a single clear focal point, or is the image cluttered?

## B. Facial Expression & Emotional Hook

Humans are naturally drawn to expressive faces that signal strong emotions. MrBeast uses faces as "emotional hooks."

- Is there a face visible? (Thumbnails with 1 face tend to perform best)
- Is the expression exaggerated and clearly readable at small sizes?
- Does the face convey a strong emotion (excitement, surprise, shock, joy)?
- **Key finding:** MrBeast's team found that a **closed-mouth smile** led to higher watch time than open-mouth expressions after A/B testing ~30 videos.

## C. High-Contrast Colors

MrBeast uses bright yellows, intense reds, and vivid blues that pop against YouTube's white interface. Contrasting colors grab attention in visually dense feeds.

- Does the thumbnail use bold, saturated colors?
- Is there strong contrast between foreground and background?
- Would this stand out in a sea of other thumbnails on the YouTube homepage?

## D. Minimal Text, Maximum Impact

MrBeast uses concise, bold text that adds intrigue without crowding. Phrases like "$100,000" or "Survived 24 Hours" — short and impactful.

- Does the thumbnail have 0-2 words? (Analysis of 100 MrBeast thumbnails shows 0 or 2 words perform best)
- Is any text large, bold, and readable at small sizes?
- Does the text add curiosity/intrigue rather than just describe the content?

## E. Curiosity & Intrigue

Present a teaser without revealing the full story. The thumbnail should compel viewers to click to find out more.

- Does the image create a question in the viewer's mind?
- Is there an element of mystery, stakes, or surprise?
- Does it withhold just enough to make you want to click?

## F. Branding & Consistency

Consistency helps viewers recognize your content in-stream. MrBeast's team decided in 2019 to put Jimmy's face in every thumbnail for brand recognition.

- Is the creator's face or a recognizable brand element present?
- Does it match the visual style of the channel's other thumbnails?

## G. Trust & Deliverability

Trust is what makes people come back and click on your videos. The thumbnail must deliver on what it promises.

- Does the thumbnail accurately represent the video content?
- Would a viewer feel misled after watching?
- Does it align with what the video intro would show? (Appleby says the thumbnail should directly align with the video intro)
