# Scoring & Calibration Guide

Reference guide for managers completing performance reviews. Use this to ensure consistent, evidence-based ratings.

## OV Score Formula

```
OV = (Performance × 0.50) + (Potential × 0.25) + (LP Score × 0.25)
```

## Performance Rating (1-10) — Weight: 50%

Evaluate results delivered against expectations for the employee's role and level.

| Rating | Score | Definition | Evidence examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| Exceptional | 9-10 | Consistently exceeded expectations; outsized impact | Delivered ahead of schedule, solved problems beyond their scope, measurable impact on business metrics |
| Strong | 7-8 | Met all expectations, exceeded in some areas | Reliable delivery, some standout contributions, positive peer feedback on output |
| Solid | 5-6 | Met expectations for the role | Completed assigned work at expected quality, no major gaps or standout moments |
| Developing | 3-4 | Met some expectations; improvement needed | Missed deadlines, quality issues, needed more support than expected for level |
| Below Expectations | 1-2 | Did not meet expectations | Significant missed deliverables, quality problems, peer feedback reflects concern |

**Calibration check:** If you're giving a 9-10 to more than 20% of your team, you're likely inflating. Exceptional means *exceptional*.

## Potential Rating (1-10) — Weight: 25%

Evaluate growth trajectory and readiness for increased scope.

| Rating | Score | Definition | Signals |
|---|---|---|---|
| High Potential | 9-10 | Ready for promotion within 1-2 quarters | Actively operating above their level, sought out by others for leadership, learning velocity is high |
| Growth Potential | 5-8 | Developing toward next level | Shows curiosity, takes on stretch assignments, improving quarter over quarter |
| Well-Placed | 1-4 | Strong in current role | Deep expertise in their domain, valuable where they are, may not aspire to or be ready for more scope |

**Important:** Well-Placed is not negative. A team of all "High Potential" people would be chaotic. You need depth and stability too.

## Leadership Principles Rating — Weight: 25%

Rate each of the five principles, then convert to a numerical score.

| Rating | Points | What it means | Frequency target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Role Model (RM) | 10 | Exemplary; others should learn from them | ~5% of team per principle |
| Demonstrates (D) | 6 | Consistently shows this in their work | Most of the team |
| Developing (DV) | 3 | Inconsistent or needs growth | A few per team |

**LP Score = average points across all 6 principles**

Example: RM, D, D, D, D, DV → (10 + 6 + 6 + 6 + 6 + 3) / 6 = **6.17**

### The six principles

1. **Ownership** — Takes responsibility for outcomes, leads up and down, proactively solves problems without being asked. Never says "not my job."

2. **Transparency** — Communicates openly and honestly, shares context, surfaces problems early. Builds trust through candor.

3. **Customer Obsession** — Starts with the customer and works backward. Deeply understands user needs and earns their trust.

4. **Strive for Excellency** — Relentlessly high bar. Continuously raises standards for products, services, and processes.

5. **Be Curious and Open Minded** — Never done learning, seeks self-improvement. Explores new possibilities and acts on them.

6. **Drive Results** — Focuses on key inputs, delivers with the right quality and timeliness. Rises to the occasion despite setbacks.

## Stack Rank Tiers

| Tier | OV Range | Target % | Implications |
|---|---|---|---|
| Top Tier (TT) | 8.5-10.0 | 20% | Priority for promotions, top compensation increases, stretch assignments |
| Highly Valued 3 (HV3) | 7.5-8.4 | 15% | Strong compensation increases, development opportunities |
| Highly Valued 2 (HV2) | 6.0-7.4 | 25% | Standard compensation increases, continued growth support |
| Highly Valued 1 (HV1) | 4.0-5.9 | 35% | Focused development plan to help reach next level |
| Least Effective (LE) | 1.0-3.9 | 5% | Requires immediate improvement; PIP required |

## Common calibration mistakes

1. **Central tendency** — Rating everyone 5-7. If no one is exceptional and no one is struggling, the ratings aren't honest.
2. **Recency bias** — Weighting the last 2-3 weeks over the full quarter. Look at the whole body of work.
3. **Halo effect** — One strong trait inflating all scores. Someone can be an exceptional coder (high performance) but poor at collaboration (low LP on that principle).
4. **Inflation** — Giving high scores because you like the person or want to avoid a hard conversation. This is the most harmful mistake — it robs people of honest feedback.
5. **Comparison anchoring** — Rating someone based on who came before them in the review cycle rather than against role expectations.
